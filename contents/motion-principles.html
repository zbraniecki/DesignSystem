Motion Principles

Firefox motion is quick, supportive and whimsical.

Story

Quick, supportive, and whimsical are the three principles that guide the motion design language of Firefox products. Following these principles, we aim to achieve a cohesive, enjoyable, and consistent experience within the family of Firefox products.

Quick

Animations should be fast and nimble and never keep the user waiting longer than they need to. The aim is to prioritise user perceived performance over technical benchmarks.

Supportive

Motion should help ease the user through the experience. It should aid the flow of actions, giving clear guidance for user orientation: spatial or temporal.

Whimsical

Animations should have a playful and Delightful personality when appropriate.

Curve

A rigidly linear movement looks unnatural to the human eye. Accelerate and decelerate smoothly helps your animation to feel more natural.

Animation curve

In Firefox we use this curve for the majority of animations. Acceleration and deceleration occur asymmetrically to feel natural.

We quickly accelerate at the beginning of the animation and slowly decelerate by the end.

Durations

Animations should last from 150ms up to 250ms. They have to feel faster and responsive. Shorter animations are easy to be compiled by machines which help to achieve a more rapid feeling.
